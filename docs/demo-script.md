# Demo Script

Imagine this. You are getting ready to leave for school or work, and your bike chain slips. Or a family member goes to make lunch and the toaster will not stay down. In a few seconds, you do not know if the fix is simple, safe, or something that needs a pro.

That is where FixIt WA 03 starts.

First, I choose the item. The app asks a few safety questions and gives a clear result: green, yellow, or red. If it is safe for a beginner, I get a short guide with clear steps. If it is not safe, the app points me to local repair help instead of guessing.

Next, I show the calculator. It compares the repair cost with the replacement cost, so you can see whether fixing it saves real money. Then I open the impact dashboard, which tracks money saved and waste avoided over time.

This matters here because Southwest Washington has a strong repair culture, small businesses that fix things, and students who may want to learn the trades. FixIt WA 03 helps people keep good things in use longer, spend less, cut waste, and support local repair work.
